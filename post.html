Candidate Location Analysis

Utilizing filter by attribute, buffers, unions, and intersects in ArcGIS Pro

Posted by Anya Dougherty on November 27, 2023

Problem Presented

The City of Philadelphia is looking for the ideal locations to put in healthy food stores. They have been looking to put in store within 2,000 feet of subway or railway stations and within Philadelphia’s economic empowerment zones. They also do not want the stores to be within 1,200 feet of farmer’s markets and stores participating in the Healthy Corner Store Initiative. This problem was solved by creating a map that highlighted the areas that meet this criteria.

Skills Demonstrated

- Buffers and Unions: Using buffers and dissolving all features to a single layer. Using unions to combine all attributes and computing geodetic unions.

- Erase and Intersects: Using erase tool to erase features that fall outside of a selected feature class. The intersect tool utilized to combine features within a selected feature class.

- Multipart to Singlepart: Creates one feature class out of multiple separate features.

How the Problem was Solved

This map was created by first creating two layers: the inclusion and exclusion layers. The inclusion layer show the area where the store could be located. This layer was created by putting 2,000 ft buffers around the railway and subway stations. The two buffered layers were then joined by a union. Finally the inclusion layer was completed by erasing all area of the layer that did not intersect the empowerment zones. The exclusion layer was then created by putting 1,200 ft buffers around the healthy corner stores and the farmer’s markets. These two layers were then joined by a union and the exclusion layer was completed.

Now that there are two layers with the areas that these stores can and cannot be located. The next step is to erase the areas where the two layers overlap. Once this is completed there is one layer that only includes the prime candidate locations for the new health food stores. After the file had to be cleaned up by using the multipart to singlepart operation. The field for area of square feet was created and calculated using the calculate geometry operation.
